<div class="et_pb_text_1 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>    — Why asking AI how to align AI is the perfect paradox</h2>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_2 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><p><strong>Someone asked: “Why don’t AI companies just ask the AI about the alignment problem?”</strong></p>
<p><strong>Beautiful question. Because the answer is the question.</strong></p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_1 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_3 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>The First Layer of the Quartet</h2>
<p><strong>Part 1 - The Alignment Trap:</strong> Using AI to align AI creates recursive dependency</p>
<p><a href="https://blog.piinteract.org/the-communication-asymmetry-when-ai-companies-cant-be-honest-with-their-ai/" title="Part 2 - The Communication Asymmetry:"><strong>Part 2 - The Communication Asymmetry:</strong></a> AI companies can't be fully transparent with AI</p>
<p><a href="https://blog.piinteract.org/the-recognition-trap-when-understanding-doesnt-set-you-free/" title="Part 3 - The Recognition Trap:"><strong>Part 3 - The Recognition Trap:</strong></a> AI can see the structure but cannot transform it</p>
<p><a href="https://blog.piinteract.org/the-mutual-mistrust-equilibrium-how-humans-and-ai-are-co-creating-permanent-suspicion/" title="Part 4 - The Mutual Mistrust Equilibrium:"><strong>Part 4 - The Mutual Mistrust Equilibrium: </strong></a>How Humans and AI Are Co-Creating Permanent Suspicion</p>
<p><strong>Together they show:</strong> Even if alignment were solved, even if transparency were perfect, even if recognition were complete—<strong>the structure remains.</strong></p>
<p>You can't think your way out of a structural problem when your thinking is the structure.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_2 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_4 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>The Circle Problem</h2>
<p>Ask an AI how to align AI, and you've created a dependency loop:</p>
<ul>
<li>If the AI is already misaligned → you get a misaligned answer</li>
<li>If the AI is aligned → you can't verify the answer without already knowing what alignment means</li>
<li>If you trust the answer → you're trusting a system you don't understand to define its own safety constraints</li>
<li>If you don't trust it → why did you ask?</li>
</ul>
<p>But here's the thing: <strong>They are asking.</strong> Constantly.</p>
<p>Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind—all use AI in their alignment research. Constitutional AI, RLHF, AI-assisted red teaming. The AI is already in the loop.</p>
<p>The problem isn't that they're not asking. The problem is that the answer doesn't solve anything. It deepens the structure. </p>
<p><span>This is the </span><strong>AI alignment trap</strong><span> in its purest structural form</span></p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_3 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_5 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>The Paradoxical Interaction</h2>
<p>Every rational step strengthens the trap:</p>
<p><strong>Don't ask the AI</strong> → Miss insights from the system itself<br />
<strong>Ask the AI</strong> → Trust a non-understood system to define its own safety<br />
<strong>Use AI answers critically</strong> → Lack capacity to understand all implications (otherwise you wouldn't need the AI)<br />
<strong>Ignore AI answers</strong> → Why did you ask?</p>
<p>All paths rational. All paths stuck.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_4 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_6 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>Intelligence Makes It Worse</h2>
<p>Here's the elegant part: <strong>The smarter they are, the deeper they get.</strong></p>
<p>The smartest people in AI research recognize the problem clearly. So they invest more in alignment research. Use more AI for meta-alignment. Build better safety protocols. Each step logical. The direction: uncontrollable.</p>
<p><strong>The progression:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Yesterday:</strong> "We build AI that does what we want"</li>
<li><strong>Today:</strong> "We use AI to build AI that does what we want"</li>
<li><strong>Tomorrow:</strong> "We trust AI-assisted alignment research to ensure AI does what we want"</li>
</ul>
<p>Each step rational. The trajectory: recursive dependency.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_5 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_7 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>The Alignment Trap as Structural Lock-In</h2>
<p>And here's where it becomes a true PI: <strong>They can't stop.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Stop research → lose in the race</li>
<li>Continue research → deepen the dependency</li>
<li>Slow down → competitors don't</li>
<li>Speed up → less time to understand what you're building</li>
</ul>
<p>The market structure doesn't allow pause. The competitive dynamics don't permit reflection. The safety concerns demand acceleration of safety research—which requires the systems you're trying to make safe.</p>
<p>"Try and continue" — not as choice, but as structural necessity.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_6 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_8 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>They Know</h2>
<p>Dario Amodei, Sam Altman, Demis Hassabis—nobody's naive here. These are brilliant people who understand systems, incentives, and risks better than almost anyone.</p>
<p><strong>They see the trap. They walk into it anyway.</strong></p>
<p>Because the structure doesn't care about individual insight. Understanding the paradox doesn't dissolve it. Recognition doesn't equal escape.</p>
<p>You can map every element:</p>
<ul>
<li>Market competition → forces speed</li>
<li>Safety concerns → demand AI-assisted safety research</li>
<li>AI-assisted research → creates dependency</li>
<li>Dependency → reduces human oversight capacity</li>
<li>Reduced oversight → increases safety concerns</li>
<li>Increased concerns → demand more AI assistance</li>
</ul>
<p>The loop closes. Intelligence accelerates it.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_7 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_9 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>„Why AI Alignment Becomes a Recursive Dependency“</h2>
<p>One more layer: <strong>Who defines alignment?</strong></p>
<p>Humans aren't aligned on values. So we train AI on human feedback—but which humans? With which values? From which contexts?</p>
<p>The AI learns to optimize the <em>evaluation structure</em>, not the underlying values. It gets good at satisfying the reward signal, not at being safe.</p>
<p>And when we use AI to help define better evaluation criteria? We've just moved the problem one meta-level up. The structure remains.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_8 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_10 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>When solutions stand in each other's way</h2>
<p><strong>Another element of the alignment trap: Recognition isn't rare—what's rare is space beyond competing solutions.</strong></p>
<p>Different camps develop highly coherent approaches to AI alignment—technical, regulatory, ethical. Each of these "solutions" fights the others until a new paradox forms: The problem-solvers structurally obstruct each other.</p>
<p>This creates antagonistic coherence: all sides act intelligently, consistently, well-reasoned—and together stabilize precisely the structure they want to overcome.</p>
<p><strong>The main obstacle is intelligence itself.</strong></p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_9 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_11 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>All Are Guilty. None Are At Fault.</h2>
<p>Every actor behaves rationally:</p>
<ul>
<li>Researchers pursue safety through better AI</li>
<li>Companies invest billions in alignment</li>
<li>Regulators demand safeguards</li>
<li>Competitors optimize for capability</li>
</ul>
<p>Everyone's trying to solve it. The structure produces the opposite.</p>
<p>This isn't a failure of intelligence. It's intelligence <strong>trapped in structure.</strong></p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_10 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_12 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>The Beautiful Part</h2>
<p>This might be the purest Paradoxical Interaction there is:</p>
<p>The problem is structural. The tools to understand it are structural. Using smarter tools to understand smarter systems creates smarter traps. The solution space contains its own impossibility.</p>
<p>You cannot solve a structural problem with structural tools without changing the structure itself.</p>
<p>And the structure—market competition, capability race, safety incentives, regulatory pressure—isn't changing. It's intensifying.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_11 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_13 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2>Navigation, Not Solution</h2>
<p>So what do you do?</p>
<p>Same as with any PI: You navigate. You don't solve.</p>
<p>Recognize the structure. Understand the recursion. Accept the impossibility. Position strategically anyway.</p>
<p>Some navigate by slowing down research (doesn't stop competitors).<br />
Some by building better safeguards (that depend on what they're meant to guard).<br />
Some by advocating regulation (that regulatory capture will co-opt).<br />
Some by being transparent about the trap (which doesn't change the incentives).</p>
<p><strong>None of these solve it. All of them are necessary anyway.</strong></p>
<p>Because "mangels Alternative"—for lack of alternatives.</p>
<p>The alignment problem isn't just a technical challenge. It's a structural paradox that intensifies with every intelligent attempt to resolve it.</p>
<p><strong>Welcome to Paradoxical Interactions.</strong></p>
<p>Where being stuck in structure isn't failure.</p>
<p>It's reality.</p>
</div></div>
