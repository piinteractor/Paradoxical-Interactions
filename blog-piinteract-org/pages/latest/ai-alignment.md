AI Alignment as Paradoxical Interaction

When the smartest people work on the hardest problem—and intelligence makes it worse

The Problem

AI alignment is the attempt to ensure that artificial intelligence systems do what humans intend them to do.

It sounds like an engineering problem. It isn't.

It is a structural problem — and structural problems don't yield to engineering. They yield to navigation. Or they don't yield at all.

Here is the structure:

The more capable an AI system becomes, the more important alignment becomes. The more important alignment becomes, the more resources flow into alignment research. The more resources flow into alignment research, the more capable AI systems become — because capability and alignment research share the same infrastructure, the same talent, the same institutions.

No one designed this. Everyone is contributing to it rationally.

All are guilty. None are at fault.

Why Intelligence Doesn't Help

The standard response to a hard problem is: think harder. Hire smarter people. Allocate more resources. Run more experiments.

This works for most problems. It is the wrong tool for structural problems.

A structural problem is one where the solution mechanism is part of the problem. Where applying more intelligence deepens the trap — because the intelligence itself is structurally compromised.

AI alignment is this kind of problem.

The researchers are brilliant. The intentions are serious. The methods are rigorous. And the structure tightens with every advance.

This is not pessimism. It is structural analysis. The difference matters — because pessimism suggests giving up, while structural analysis suggests navigating differently.

What This Section Does

This section does not offer solutions. PI doesn't offer solutions. It offers structural clarity — which is the only thing that makes navigation possible.

Two angles:

AI Alignment Traps — The intelligent, well-intentioned, structurally rational attempts to solve alignment. Each one coherent. Each one a step deeper into the structure. The four horsemen of a very specific apocalypse.

Systemic Immunization — What remains possible when solutions are not. How systems develop structural resistance — not to the problem, but to the illusion that the problem can be solved from within.

Paradoxical Interactions (PI): When rational actors consistently produce collectively irrational outcomes — not through failure, but through structure.

All are guilty. None are at fault.
