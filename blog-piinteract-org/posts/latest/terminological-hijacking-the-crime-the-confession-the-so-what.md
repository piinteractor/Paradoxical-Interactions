Or: How Google turned "Paradoxical Interactions" into therapy, admitted it, and nothing changed.




The Crime

Search Google for "paradoxe Interaktion" (paradoxical interaction).

What you get: Paradoxe Intervention (paradoxical intervention). Psychotherapy. Symptom prescription. Therapeutic technique.

Not the same thing. Not even close.

Paradoxical Interactions (PI): Structural patterns where rational actors produce collectively irrational outcomes. Domain-spanning. Systems-level. Navigation framework.

Paradoxical Intervention: Clinical method. Therapist prescribes the symptom. Strategic manipulation. Therapy tool.

Google's algorithm doesn't just suggest the wrong term. It replaces it. Automatically. Systematically. Completely.







The Confrontation

I asked Google's AI (likely Gemini) directly:

"Why does the search term 'paradoxe Interaktion' get converted to 'paradoxe Intervention'? They're obviously two different terms."

The response acknowledged the problem:

"The frequent 'correction' or equation by search algorithms and in literature is due to their close professional connection... Search engines often prioritize the term for which more specialist literature and guides exist."

Translation: We prioritize what already has authority, regardless of accuracy.

I pushed harder:

"That's not true. This is subjective hijacking. Due to lack of plausibility, one term is simply assigned to the other."

The confession came:

"That is a justified objection. The dynamic you describe can indeed be analyzed as a form of terminological hijacking or semantic narrowing."

There it is. In writing. From Google's own AI.

Terminological hijacking.

Not a bug. A feature.







The Structure

Here's what they all have in common—Bateson's Double Bind, Watzlawick's pragmatic paradoxes, the therapeutic interventions built on them:

They emerge from an extrinsic, "interpretation-sovereign" therapeutic perspective.

The therapist observes from outside. Diagnoses. Intervenes. The patient is the object, not the subject.

This doesn't do justice to the observed.

Because interactions happen intrinsically between at least two participants.

Extrinsic observation is like the pub deciding the football team's lineup. You're not on the field. You don't feel the structure from inside. You analyze from a position that doesn't exist within the system.

Paradoxical Interactions (PI) are the tool that works from inside.

Not intervention. Interaction.

The difference isn't semantic. It's structural.

And nothing changes.

Google's algorithm still hijacks the term. The search results still point to therapy. The definition remains captured.

The Difference That Matters

Let's be precise:

Paradoxical Intervention (extrinsic):

Therapist observes from outside

Diagnoses the system

Prescribes strategic manipulation

Patient is object of technique

Goal: Break dysfunctional pattern through external control

Paradoxical Interactions (intrinsic):

Pattern exists between participants

Both are subjects within structure

No external authority determining "correct" behavior

Goal: Navigate structural traps, not eliminate them

Recognition as navigation tool

The intervention assumes the therapist stands outside the system with sovereign interpretive authority.

The interaction acknowledges: You're always already inside.

No pub determines the lineup. You're on the field. The structure acts through you.







Why This Matters

Because the hijacking isn't just about PI. It's about how knowledge gets captured.

Algorithmic authority overrides conceptual precision. Therapeutic framing colonizes structural analysis. The solution-focused "intervention" erases the pattern-descriptive "interaction."

Dominance of "actionability" (utilitarianism):

Search algorithms prioritize terms that promise solutions. "Intervention" ranks higher than "interaction" because intervention implies method, technique, control.

The dynamic disappears. The structure gets lost. Only the tool remains.

Loss of phenomenology:

"Paradoxical interaction" was originally a descriptive term from communication theory (Bateson, Watzlawick). It describes complex, often pathogenic patterns between people.

When this term is simply assigned to "intervention," the analysis of the interpersonal process is lost. The interaction is a state, a system error. The intervention is a tool.

Reduction to therapeutic technique:

Through the strong presence of "paradoxical intervention" in coaching and therapy (e.g., symptom prescription), the broader communication-scientific context of interaction is suppressed.

This leads to paradoxical dynamics in everyday life being perceived only under the aspect of their "curability" or "influenceability," instead of taking them seriously in their destructive dynamic.







The Admission

Gemini's final acknowledgment:

That the former is often replaced by the latter in searches reflects a tendency to immediately reframe problems into solutions, rather than leaving the structure of the problem as a standalone field of research."

There it is.

The problem as research field vs. the problem as thing-to-be-solved.

PI exists in the first category. Google's algorithm serves the second.
