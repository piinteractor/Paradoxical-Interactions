<div class="et_pb_text_4 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2 class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-base first:mt-0" id="the-newsfeed-party">The Newsfeed Party</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">It is a birthday party that never ends.<br />There is always a cake somewhere, always a beach, always a promotion, always someone with better light and better teeth.<br />You open the app in a quiet kitchen at 23:17, still in the clothes from work, the sink full, your head buzzing from the meeting that went nowhere.<br />On the screen, nobody has dishes. Nobody has a headache. Nobody is stuck in a loop with their boss.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">You scroll.<br />You see a friend posting a sunset with a caption about gratitude. Another one runs a marathon. Someone you barely know holds a microphone on a conference stage.<br />You hesitate for a second over the empty status field, then close the app.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The party is full. Nobody is actually there.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_2 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_5 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2 class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-lg first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4" id="the-highlight-carousel">The Highlight Carousel</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The core metaphor is simple: social media is a never-ending highlight reel that pretends to be a documentary.<br />In a documentary, you see the pauses, the failures, the awkward silences between scenes.<br />In the highlight reel, only the peaks survive. Everything else is cut in the edit room.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Posting works like editing.<br />You stand in front of a messy day and select the one frame that looks like a life worth having.<br />You crop the background, mute the noise, adjust the light.<br />You do this not because you are dishonest, but because you know what the system rewards.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The feed itself becomes a hall of mirrors.<br />Everyone looks at everyone else’s best fifteen seconds and quietly recalibrates their sense of normal.<br />Nobody tells you to fake it. The structure just pays you in tiny rewards every time you do.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The highlight carousel doesn’t need a mastermind. It only needs a button that says “like”.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_3 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_6 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2 class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-lg first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4" id="zuckerbergs-clean-glass">Zuckerberg’s Clean Glass</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The ZDF big-tech documentaries spend a lot of time on Meta’s scandals, but the more subtle story is the surface itself.<span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="group/trigger inline-flex min-w-0" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline"></span></span><br />Facebook and Instagram offer a polished, clean pane of glass that always looks the same, whether you are celebrating a wedding or hiding a depression.<span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="inline-flex" aria-label="20 Jahre Facebook: Von Fake News, Datenschutz und Meinungsmacht | ZDFinfo Doku" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline" data-pplx-citation="" data-pplx-citation-url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xCHxug5_8gM" rel="nofollow noopener"></span>​</span><span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="inline-flex" aria-label="Weltmacht Facebook - Das Reich des Mark Zuckerberg - ZDF" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline" data-pplx-citation="" data-pplx-citation-url="https://www.zdf.de/video/dokus/zdf-info-100/weltmacht-facebook--das-reich-des-mark-zuckerberg-100" rel="nofollow noopener"><a rel="noopener" class="inline-flex max-w-full min-w-0" target="_blank" href="https://www.zdf.de/video/dokus/zdf-info-100/weltmacht-facebook--das-reich-des-mark-zuckerberg-100"></a></span>​</span></p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Mark Zuckerberg talks about connection, community, bringing the world closer together.<span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="inline-flex" aria-label="Mark Zuckerberg - Tech-Genie auf Trump-Kurs" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline" data-pplx-citation="" data-pplx-citation-url="https://www.zdf.de/video/dokus/zdf-info-100/zdfinfo-marc-zuckerberg-tech-genie-auf-trump-kurs-100" rel="nofollow noopener"><a rel="noopener" class="inline-flex max-w-full min-w-0" target="_blank" href="https://www.zdf.de/video/dokus/zdf-info-100/zdfinfo-marc-zuckerberg-tech-genie-auf-trump-kurs-100"></a></span>​</span><span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="inline-flex" aria-label="20 Jahre Facebook: Von Fake News, Datenschutz und Meinungsmacht | ZDFinfo Doku" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline" data-pplx-citation="" data-pplx-citation-url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xCHxug5_8gM" rel="nofollow noopener"></span>​</span><br />The interface says the same thing: everything is shareable, everything is relatable, everything can be reacted to by the same three little icons.<br />Birth, breakup, burnout and breakfast all flow through the same visual template.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">On the corporate side, Meta optimizes for engagement, time-on-site, and ad inventory.<span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="group/trigger inline-flex min-w-0" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline"></span></span><br />Posts that are easy to process, emotionally simple, and quickly rewarding perform better.<span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="inline-flex" aria-label="20 Jahre Facebook: Von Fake News, Datenschutz und Meinungsmacht | ZDFinfo Doku" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline" data-pplx-citation="" data-pplx-citation-url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xCHxug5_8gM" rel="nofollow noopener"></span>​</span><br />Complexity, ambiguity, unresolved threads — they produce confusion, not clicks.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">So the algorithm learns to prefer the fake paradise.<br />It suppresses the complicated life not because it is evil, but because the numbers say the highlight reel is more profitable.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The result: a CEO can honestly claim to connect people, while the system steadily erases the parts of life that would make connection real.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_4 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_7 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2 class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-lg first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4" id="the-structural-turn-dissonance-on-autopilot">The Structural Turn: Dissonance on Autopilot</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">This is where cognitive dissonance enters.<br />You know your life is not a highlight reel.<br />You see that everyone else’s life looks like one.<br />Your brain has to choose where the lie sits.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">One option: “Their lives are fake.”<br />That is hard to maintain over thousands of posts, especially when you know some people offline.<br />The other option: “My life is the outlier.”<br />That one hurts, but it is structurally easier. The platform helps by constantly delivering new evidence.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The second dissonance sits deeper: you do not actually care about most of what you are shown.<br />You click “like” on a colleague’s engagement, swipe past a distant cousin’s baby, skim through a stranger’s vacation.<br />You perform attention without feeling it.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Meanwhile, everyone else does the same for you.<br />The metrics go up.<br />The sense of being seen goes down.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The system reduces dissonance by lowering the bar for what counts as “connection”.<br />A tap replaces a conversation. A reaction replaces a response.<br />The less real the interaction, the easier it is to keep the perfect-world illusion intact.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The structure does not need you to lie.<br />It just needs you to ignore everything that cannot be compressed into a clean rectangle.</p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_5 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_8 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2><!-- obsidian --></h2>
<h2 class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-lg first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4" id="the-perfect-world-pi">The Perfect-World PI</h2>
<p class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-lg first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4">The more a platform optimizes for shareable, pleasant, easily consumable moments, the more it must structurally hide the messy complexity of real lives — until “connection” becomes a performance of caring without actual attention.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2"><strong>Everyone acts rationally:</strong></p>
<ul class="marker:text-quiet list-disc">
<li class="py-0 my-0 prose-p:pt-0 prose-p:mb-2 prose-p:my-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:pt-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:mb-2 [&amp;&gt;p]:my-0">
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">User — shows only their best sides, because they fear judgment and know that positive, simple posts get more visibility and approval.</p>
</li>
<li class="py-0 my-0 prose-p:pt-0 prose-p:mb-2 prose-p:my-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:pt-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:mb-2 [&amp;&gt;p]:my-0">
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Other users — react superficially to dozens of posts, because deep attention is impossible at scale and the interface makes shallow responses effortless.</p>
</li>
<li class="py-0 my-0 prose-p:pt-0 prose-p:mb-2 prose-p:my-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:pt-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:mb-2 [&amp;&gt;p]:my-0">
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Platform (Meta) — promotes surface-friendly, emotionally straightforward content, because it maximizes engagement, growth and advertising revenue.<span class="citation-nbsp"></span><span class="group/trigger inline-flex min-w-0" data-state="closed"><span class="citation inline"></span></span></p>
</li>
<li class="py-0 my-0 prose-p:pt-0 prose-p:mb-2 prose-p:my-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:pt-0 [&amp;&gt;p]:mb-2 [&amp;&gt;p]:my-0">
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Advertisers — pay for placements next to clean, aspirational images, not next to unresolved grief, burnout or conflict.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2"><strong>Outcome — structural, not intended:</strong><br />The feed fills up with curated fragments of “heile Welt”, while complex, demanding or painful realities vanish from perception.<br />The platform looks like a connected, thriving community, but users quietly feel more isolated, more average, more replaceable.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2"><em>All are guilty. None are at fault.</em></p>
</div></div>

<div class="et_pb_divider_6 et_pb_divider et_pb_space et_pb_divider_position_center et_pb_module"><div class="et_pb_divider_internal"></div></div>

<div class="et_pb_text_9 et_pb_text et_pb_bg_layout_light et_pb_module et_flex_module"><div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h2><!-- obsidian --></h2>
<h2 class="font-editorial font-bold mb-2 mt-4 [.has-inline-images_&amp;]:clear-end text-lg first:mt-0 md:text-lg [hr+&amp;]:mt-4" id="navigation-in-the-fake-paradise">Navigation in the Fake Paradise</h2>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">There is no way to “use it right” in the sense of fixing the structure.<br />You can only change how you see what it does to you.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">One option is to treat the feed as advertising, not as a mirror.<br />Everything you see is an edited pitch for a version of a life, including your own.<br />That does not make it worthless, but it makes it less sacred.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Another option is to add friction.<br />Fewer posts, longer texts, uglier photos, slower replies.<br />You will lose reach. You will gain signal.<br />The algorithm will quietly demote you. The people who remain will be the ones who can live with a crack in the glass.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">Most important: stop expecting the system to carry what it structurally cannot carry — grief, ambivalence, contradiction, the unspectacular middle of the curve.<br />Those things still need different spaces, different tempos, different risks.</p>
<p class="my-2 [&amp;+p]:mt-4 [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:inline-block [&amp;_strong:has(+br)]:pb-2">The fake paradise was built to be scrolled, not to be lived in.<br />The only real freedom inside is to stop mistaking its applause for proof that you exist.</p>
</div></div>
